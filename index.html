Biotech Breakthroughs: Is Race Biologically Real?

What I Did and Learned (Claim & Reasoning Part 1)

In new media class, we did a real lab experiment to answer a question Is race real from a biological perspective? Some people think humans are divided into distinct biological groups that can be identified just by looking at skin color or hair texture. However, after studying our own DNA, my Claim is that race is not biologically real. We learned that all humans are incredibly similar on the inside, and visible traits like skin tone are just small adaptations, not entirely different categories of people.

Our Evidence & Scientific Reasoning (Evidence & Reasoning Part 2)

For our Evidence, we swabbed the inside of our cheeks to collect our own DNA. Then, we used a PCR machine to make millions of copies of a specific DNA segment so we could look at it closely. When we checked our results on the gel to look for DNA matches, the data showed that students of different backgrounds shared the exact same DNA fragments. My Reasoning is that if biological race were real, people of the same race would have the exact same matching DNA patterns, but our PCR experiment proved that we share genetic matches with classmates of all different races.

My 21st Century Skill

The skill I used the most was Critical Thinking (Problem Solving). Working with the PCR machine and loading the tiny DNA samples into the gel lanes took a lot of focus. I had to pay close attention to our lab steps and look at the final DNA match bands carefully to find the scientific truth about human DNA.
