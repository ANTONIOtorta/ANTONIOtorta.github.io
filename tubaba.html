ANTONIO's SHARED CAnVAS SCREEN PRINTING PROJECT

In my new media tech class, our screen printing project bridged traditional craftsmanship with digital design workflows. Using vector graphics software, we first created layered, high-contrast images optimized for stencil exposurevexperimenting with halftones and duotones. After burning our designs onto screens, we printed them onto various substrates, from cotton to boxes, exploring how texture and transparency affect digital aesthetics in person. This process not only taught me new mechanics of analog but also how screen printing can critique digital culture, turning pixel-based imperfections into tactile, representations of real life.

The Process

1. The Emblem (Digital Prep)

Creating high-contrast film positives. Black ink blocks the UV light.

2. The Exposure

Emulsion bakes under UV light. Unexposed areas wash away, creating the stencil.

3. The Setup

Registering the screen on the press. Precision is everything.

4. The Pull

Flooding the screen and pulling the squeegee at a 45-degree angle.
